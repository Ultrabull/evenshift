? How EvenShift works

EvenShift builds a fair patient assignment for the shift. Score each patient once, tap Generate, adjust if you like.

Score tab. Tap any room and mark what the patient needs. It adds up the points and divides by your unit's number to give the acuity. Use Quick for the usual stuff, Full list for everything, or Express to just set the number. Charge can change the factors and the scale under ⋯ → Edit acuity rubric.

The acuity number is nursing hours — higher means more work. A 5+ is a heavy patient, a 2 is light.

Board tab. Tap Generate. It evens out patient counts first, then nursing hours to within half an hour, keeps each nurse in one hall (or two halls on the same wing), spreads feeds, draws, drips and discharges around, and keeps new grads off the heaviest and behavioral patients. Tap a room, then a nurse, to move anyone.

The floor is four halls off the central station — two on the left wing, two on the right. The board keeps each nurse tight to the station and never splits a nurse across wings unless evening the hours leaves no choice.

Balance pill. Up top it reads green when every nurse is within about half an hour of the others.

Next shift → keeps each patient's acuity and notes but clears the assignment, so the next charge starts from where you left off instead of re-scoring everyone.

Flags & what they mean. ISO = isolation · D/C = discharge · ADM = admission · BEH = behavioral · Conf = confused / fall risk · Trach = tracheostomy · Drip = IV drip · INS = insulin drip · CVL = central line · Wound = wound care · Total = total care · 1:1 = sitter · PD = dialysis. 🔒 keeps a patient with the same nurse across a re-generate. The board also spreads these types out so no nurse gets a pile of one kind.
